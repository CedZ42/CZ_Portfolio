Team Report — Reverse Engineering

REVERSE ENGINEERING TEAM REPORT

In this collaborative project, our team selected an electric rotary sander and reverse engineered it to uncover its design intent, manufacturing methods, and material choices. The goal was to develop a thorough understanding of how professional engineers document and analyze existing products, which is a critical skill in competitive benchmarking, product development, and quality control.

My primary contribution focused on the assembly and disassembly documentation and the drivetrain system — recording each stage of the teardown process, capturing component relationships, the transfer of energy, and identifying the power path. By analyzing the drivetrain's layout, I identified the sequence-of-assembly logic and part interdependencies required for the device to function. I also created clear, structured documentation that allowed our team to accurately reconstruct the device and identify key design decisions made by the original manufacturer.

Hand-Drawn — Dimensioned Sketch

Stapler Teardown Drawing

This hand-drawn dimensioned drawing was produced following a physical teardown of a standard desktop stapler. Creating this document required careful measurement with calipers, application of standard dimensioning conventions (including tolerance notation and reference lines), and the ability to translate a three-dimensional object into clear orthographic views. Freehand technical sketching remains a foundational engineering skill — fast, portable, and essential for rapid design communication in the field.

Fusion 360 — CAD Technical Drawing

Jigsaw Technical Drawing

This technical drawing was generated from a 3D model of a jigsaw created in Autodesk Fusion 360. The project required proficiency in parametric modeling, accurate application of geometric dimensioning and tolerancing (GD&T) standards, and the ability to produce a clean, multi-view drawing sheet suitable for manufacturing or review. CAD-generated drawings bridge the gap between design intent and real-world fabrication — a skill increasingly demanded across all sectors of the engineering industry.
